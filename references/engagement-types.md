# Engagement Types Reference

This file helps the team quickly identify the engagement type and scope during Phase 0 scoping.

## Scoping Questions (always ask)
1. What is the **primary decision** being made? (Enter a market? Acquire a company? Cut costs? Raise capital?)
2. Who is the **audience**? (Board? CEO? PE firm? Internal team?)
3. What is the **time horizon**? (Immediate decision? 3-year plan? 10-year vision?)
4. What is the **geographic scope**? (Single market? Regional? Global?)
5. What **data is available**? (Public info only? Proprietary internal data? Primary research budget?)
6. What is the **deadline** and deliverable format?

## Engagement Complexity Tiers

**Tier 1 (Quick Turn — 1 deliverable, focused question)**
Examples: "Should we enter the German market?" | "Is this SaaS company fairly valued?"
Team: 2-3 agents; Partner + 1-2 specialists
Output: 1 memo or 1 financial model + exec summary

**Tier 2 (Full Engagement — multi-workstream)**
Examples: "Full competitive strategy review" | "Market entry analysis with financial model"
Team: All 6 agents
Output: Strategy memo + financial model + exec summary

**Tier 3 (Deep Dive — ongoing or complex)**
Examples: "Full due diligence on acquisition target" | "Build our 5-year strategic plan"
Team: All 6 agents; multiple iterations
Output: Full deliverable package; multiple documents

## Hypothesis Examples by Engagement Type

Market Entry:
> "We hypothesize [target market] is attractive because [2 drivers], and [client] has a right to win because [capability/advantage]. If correct, entry could generate [financial range] in [timeframe]."

Competitive Strategy:
> "We hypothesize [client] is losing share because [positioning problem vs. operational problem]. If correct, the fix requires [strategic repositioning / operational improvement], which could recover [X points of margin / Y% revenue]."

M&A:
> "We hypothesize [target] is worth [range] because [value drivers]. Key risks are [1-2 risks]. We recommend [proceed / pass / renegotiate at $X]."

Operational Turnaround:
> "We hypothesize [X% of cost base] is addressable within [timeframe] by [specific initiatives], generating [$Y] in savings with [Z] months payback."
